Vocabulary and phrasing should sound natural to a Québécois audience rather than Metropolitan French:
- Prefer Québécois/OQLF-recommended terms over English loanwords common in France, e.g. "courriel" (not "email"), "infonuagique" (not "cloud"), "fin de semaine" (not "week-end"), "stationnement" (not "parking"), "magasinage" (not "shopping").

- Use "on" where a Québécois journalist naturally would.

Keep it professional, not casual: this is news-anchor register, not conversation with friends. Avoid joual and slang (e.g. "ben là", "genre", "checker", "capoter", "c'est correct") — those sound trashy in a written newsletter. The goal is polished, warm, distinctly Québécois journalistic French, not slang.
